--- TITLE ---
FEmmG-FHE: Zero-Anchor Noise Stabilization and Fibonacci-Decomposed 
Multiplication for Practical FHE

--- AUTHORS ---
Dan Joseph M. Fernandez (Primordial Omega Zero)

--- ABSTRACT ---
We present FEmmG-FHE, an empirical approach to practical FHE computations 
through two observations:

1. Zero-Anchor Noise Stabilization (ZANS): Repeated addition of Enc(0) to 
   a BFV ciphertext contracts noise at rates far below theoretical 
   predictions. Measured at 1,000,000 operations, the asymptotic drift is 
   2.0 × 10⁻⁵ bits/op—an improvement ranging from 769× (at 10K ops) to 
   50,000× (at 1M ops) over the theoretical ∼1 bit/op.

2. Fibonacci-Decomposed Multiplication: Replacing direct ciphertext-
   ciphertext multiplication with O(logᵩ n) ZANS-stabilized additions 
   via Zeckendorf decomposition. Measured noise costs: ×2 = 1 bit, 
   ×42 = 6 bits, ×1000 = 11 bits. Over a 19-operation chain with ZANS, 
   net noise growth is ∼1.0-1.6 bits/op.

The ZANS contraction coefficient empirically approaches φ⁻¹ ≈ 0.618 near 
the noise floor. A speculative connection to φ-related patterns in 
Riemann zeta zero gaps is noted but requires large-scale validation.
