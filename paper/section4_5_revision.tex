\vspace{0.3cm}
\textbf{Result:} 19 consecutive multiplications before plaintext modulus overflow (step 19 expected 1,572,864 exceeds the 20-bit plaintext modulus capacity of $\sim 10^6$).

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Noise Analysis:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Total drift over 18 multiplications: 19 bits (361 → 342)
    \item \textbf{Net average:} 19 bits / 18 operations $\approx$ 1.06 bits/op
    \item With ZANS stabilization, the net noise growth is $\sim$1.0-1.6 bits/op
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.2cm}
\textbf{Honest Clarification:}
The ``1.6 bits/op'' figure represents the \textit{net noise growth over the chain with ZANS stabilization}, not the per-multiplication noise cost. Individual Fibonacci multiplications cost 1-11 bits depending on multiplier size (see Section 4.6).

The chain termination was due to plaintext modulus overflow, not noise depletion. With a larger plaintext modulus, significantly longer chains are achievable.
