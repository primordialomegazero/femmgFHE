\begin{abstract}
We explore the algebraic extension ring $R[X]/(X^2-X-1)$ where $R$ is a cyclotomic polynomial ring, as a primitive for lattice-based cryptography. The golden ratio $\varphi = (1+\sqrt{5})/2$ and its conjugate $\psi = -1/\varphi$ serve as the two roots, enabling a decomposition $R[X]/(X^2-X-1) \cong R \times R$ via the Chinese Remainder Theorem.

We investigate three applications with algorithm-level optimizations:

\textbf{Noise Management for FHE:} A fused clean operation using only 2 homomorphic additions (vs.\ 4 originally) attenuates the $\psi$-component by $\psi^2 \approx 0.382$ per cycle. Scalar multiplication detection reduces EvalMult from 4 to 2 per ciphertext multiplication. Batch processing (8 multiplications per clean cycle) achieves 1.0 amortized EvalMult per multiplication---the theoretical minimum. Combined, these optimizations yield a \textbf{3.4$\times$ throughput improvement} over the original implementation. We demonstrate 320 sequential homomorphic multiplications with $\varphi$-error growing exponentially at $\sim$1.00075 per multiplication and $\psi$-noise remaining flat at $10^{-12}$--$10^{-13}$.

\textbf{Dual-Reality Program Encoding:} Two algebraically equivalent circuit representations encoded in the $\varphi$ and $\psi$ realities produce outputs that are statistically indistinguishable (51.2\% adversary success rate, 95\% CI: 50\% $\pm$ 3.1\%). We explicitly note this is not cryptographic iO. A gate-mapping compiler handles circuits with different gate counts via identity padding (5/5 test cases verified).

\textbf{Compact Key Encapsulation:} Storing $\varphi$ and $\psi$ evaluations yields experimental KEM variants from 80 to 192 bytes. Our Level 5 variant uses Ring-LWE with a fixed public matrix---a different assumption from Kyber's Module-LWE. Size comparisons are illustrative, not claims of equivalent security.

All experiments on consumer hardware (AMD Ryzen 5 2600, 16GB RAM, WSL2). Code available at \url{https://github.com/primordialomegazero/femmgFHE}. All limitations documented honestly.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Fully Homomorphic Encryption (FHE) enables computation on encrypted data. Since Gentry's breakthrough~\cite{gentry2009}, FHE has advanced through Ring-LWE constructions~\cite{brakerski2014, fan2012} to standardized schemes like CKKS~\cite{cheon2017}. Noise accumulation during homomorphic operations remains the central challenge.

\textbf{Our Contribution.} We explore the $\varphi$-extension ring $R[X]/(X^2-X-1)$ as an algebraic primitive. The roots $\varphi \approx 1.618$ and $\psi \approx -0.618$ create asymmetric dynamics---amplification in one direction, attenuation in another. This yields:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Optimized FHE noise management.} Through fused operations, scalar multiplication detection, and batch processing, we achieve 1.0 amortized EvalMult per multiplication---the theoretical minimum for any FHE scheme.
    \item \textbf{Dual-reality program encoding.} A mechanism for plausible deniability in program representation, not general iO.
    \item \textbf{Compact KEM.} Experimental key compression using Ring-LWE with fixed public matrix.
\end{enumerate}

\textbf{What This Work Is NOT:}
\begin{itemize}
    \item Not a claim of solving FHE, iO, or PQC standardization
    \item Not a NIST submission
    \item Not third-party verified
    \item Not production-ready software
\end{itemize}

\section{Mathematical Preliminaries}

\subsection{The $\varphi$-Extension Ring}

\begin{definition}
Let $R = \mathbb{Z}_q[X]/(X^N+1)$. The $\varphi$-extension ring is $R_\varphi = R[Y]/(Y^2 - Y - 1)$ with $Y^2 = Y + 1$. The roots are $\varphi = (1+\sqrt{5})/2 \approx 1.618$ and $\psi = -1/\varphi \approx -0.618$.
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[CRT Decomposition]
\label{thm:crt}
$R[Y]/(Y^2 - Y - 1) \cong R \times R$ via $a + bY \mapsto (a + b\varphi,\; a + b\psi)$ when $\varphi - \psi = \sqrt{5}$ is invertible in $R$.
\end{theorem}

\subsection{Zero-Depth Operations}

$\text{mul}_Y(a,b) = (b, a+b)$ requires only one addition and zero EvalMult calls. Its matrix is $M = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & 1 \\ 1 & 1 \end{pmatrix}$ with eigenvalues $\varphi$ (expanding) and $\psi$ (contracting, $|\psi| < 1$).

\begin{definition}[Fused Clean Operation]
$\text{clean}(a,b) = \text{div}_Y \circ \text{mul}_Y^3(a,b) = (a+b,\; a+2b)$. Its matrix is $M^2 = \begin{pmatrix} 1 & 1 \\ 1 & 2 \end{pmatrix}$ with eigenvalues $\varphi^2 \approx 2.618$ and $\psi^2 \approx 0.382$. The fused form requires only 2 EvalAdd operations instead of 4.
\end{definition}

\subsection{Scalar Multiplication Optimization}

In our FHE loop, the multiplier is always a scalar constant $\text{mult} = (pre, 0)$. The general $\varphi$-ring multiplication $(a_1+b_1Y)(a_2+b_2Y)$ requires 4 EvalMult. However, when $b_2 = 0$, this reduces to $(a_1+b_1Y) \cdot pre = pre \cdot a_1 + pre \cdot b_1 Y$---requiring only 2 EvalMult. This is a 50\% reduction that applies to every multiplication in our FHE loop.

\subsection{Noise Dynamics}

The $\varphi$-error grows exponentially with a very small base:

\begin{equation}
    \eta_\varphi(n) \approx \eta_\varphi(0) \cdot (1.00075)^n
\end{equation}

Projecting to 1\% error yields approximately 14,000 multiplications (assuming constant growth rate and no modulus depletion).

The $\psi$-noise is attenuated by $\psi^2 \approx 0.382$ per clean cycle. After 3 clean cycles (24 multiplications with batch 8), $\psi$-noise drops from $1.46 \times 10^{-1}$ to $1.41 \times 10^{-6}$---five orders of magnitude. It stabilizes at $10^{-12}$--$10^{-13}$.

\section{Dual-Reality Program Encoding}

\subsection{Concept}

The CRT decomposition means any computation in $R_\varphi$ produces two simultaneous outputs. We encode two algebraically equivalent circuit representations such that both produce correct outputs but an observer cannot determine which was the intended one.

\textbf{This is not cryptographic iO.} It provides plausible deniability for program representation within a restricted class (algebraically equivalent polynomial evaluation trees with Catalan-number many variants).

\subsection{Gate-Mapping Compiler}

Our compiler (v2) handles circuits with different gate counts by padding the smaller circuit with identity gates that carry the output value. Verified 5/5 test cases for $(x+1)(x+2)$ (6 gates) vs.\ $x^2+3x+2$ (7 gates).

\subsection{Adversary Game}

1,000 trials. Adversary receives both $\varphi$ and $\psi$ outputs and must identify which representation was intended. Success rate: 51.2\% (95\% CI: 50\% $\pm$ 3.1\%, $z \approx 0.76$, $p \approx 0.45$ two-tailed). Cannot reject the null hypothesis of random guessing.

\section{Compact Key Encapsulation}

\subsection{Construction}

We compress Ring-LWE public keys by storing only $\text{eval}_\varphi(T)$ and $\text{eval}_\psi(T)$ instead of the full polynomial $T = A \cdot S + E$. These evaluations determine $T$ uniquely by CRT.

\subsection{Assumptions and Caveats}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Ring-LWE vs.\ Module-LWE.} Kyber uses Module-LWE with tighter worst-case reductions. We use Ring-LWE with a fixed matrix $A$. This is a different, less-studied assumption.
    \item \textbf{Fixed public matrix.} Unlike Kyber's fresh $A$ per key, our fixed $A$ means a single compromise affects all users. Formal analysis pending.
    \item \textbf{Size comparisons are illustrative.} The 192-byte KEM is not claimed equivalent to Kyber-1024's security.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}

The $\varphi$-extension ring approach differs from Gentry's bootstrapping~\cite{gentry2009} and scale-invariant schemes~\cite{brakerski2012}. Our clean operation is orthogonal to CKKS rescaling~\cite{cheon2017}. Ring extension techniques have been explored for packing efficiency~\cite{gentry2012} but not for asymmetric noise dynamics.

For program obfuscation, general-purpose iO~\cite{garg2013} requires multilinear maps. Our encoding is far more limited---it provides plausible deniability for evaluation strategy, not general circuit hiding.

For KEM, Kyber~\cite{kyber} uses Module-LWE. Our construction explores a different design point with aggressive compression. Similar compression appears in lattice signatures~\cite{ducas2014}.

\section{Limitations}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{No third-party verification.} All results author-reported.
    \item \textbf{Exponential error growth.} Base $\sim$1.00075 per multiplication. Practical limit $\sim$14,000 mults.
    \item \textbf{Not cryptographic iO.} Our encoding provides plausible deniability, not general obfuscation.
    \item \textbf{Weaker KEM assumptions.} Ring-LWE with fixed matrix vs.\ Module-LWE.
    \item \textbf{TOY parameters.} FHE at RingDim=4096/8192.
    \item \textbf{API-level cross-library only.} Operations verified, not full correctness.
    \item \textbf{Not constant-time.} Side-channel vulnerable.
    \item \textbf{Slow bootstrap.} $\sim$40s on consumer CPU.
    \item \textbf{Zeckendorf scope.} Exponentiation chains only.
    \item \textbf{No formal security reduction.} Informal CRT argument only.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Conclusion}

We explored the $\varphi$-extension ring for lattice-based cryptography. Through algorithm optimization---fused clean, scalar multiplication detection, and batch processing---we achieved 1.0 amortized EvalMult per multiplication (3.4$\times$ throughput improvement). The dual-reality encoding provides plausible deniability for program representation. The compact KEM explores the RLWE design space at 192 bytes.

All limitations are documented. We believe the $\varphi$-extension ring warrants further study, particularly its asymmetric eigenvalue dynamics. Code is public. We welcome verification and critique.
